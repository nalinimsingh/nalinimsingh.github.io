MIT HKN

I'm the 2016-2017 president of the MIT Chapter of Eta Kappa Nu (HKN).

At MIT, HKN primarily functions as a service organization that works with the EECS department to support its students. We do this by:

  • Offering a free tutoring service to undergrads
  • Publishing detailed semesterly course reviews in the Underground Guide
  • Running educational, outreach, and social events, as described in our blog

We've introduced a number of new initiatives this year, including group tutoring sessions, events to match students with research opportunities, and a redesign of one of the EECS student lounges.

Blueprint

My freshman year, a couple of friends and I wanted to help high school students learn more practical coding skills to complement the more theoretical CS concepts they learn in school.

So, we started Blueprint, where over 300 local students built web, Android, and iOS applications during a day-long event at Google Cambridge.

Blueprint has become an annual event and is now in its fourth year!
